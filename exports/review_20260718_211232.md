# Review

### Introduction
Artificial intelligence (AI) has become a transformative force in various aspects of society, from scientific research to urban analysis and governance. The rapid development and deployment of AI technologies have raised fundamental questions about their impact, regulation, and ethical implications. This literature review aims to provide an overview of the current state of research in AI, highlighting key findings, methodologies, and gaps in the field. The review is based on five recent research papers that explore different aspects of AI, including its moral attitudes, industrialization of research, misclassification of autistic writing, building typology predictions, and governance.

### Current Research
The current research in AI is diverse and multifaceted. Paper 1 by Van Belle (2026) explores the moral attitudes of sentient artificial superintelligence (ASI) towards humanity, highlighting the need for a novel inversion in AI ethics. Paper 2 by Jeannot (2026) discusses the industrialization of research, where AI is transforming scientific research into an autonomous participant in the research cycle. Paper 3 by Chambers and Kelley (2026) investigates the misclassification of autistic writing as AI-generated, revealing biases in detection models. Paper 4 by Shabrina et al. (2026) assesses the agreements between AI and human expert reasoning in building typology predictions based on street view imagery. Paper 5 by Lundgren and Tallberg (2026) examines public preferences and regulatory options for governing AI, finding a strong support for regulating AI and prioritizing safety over innovation.

### Common Methodologies
The research papers employ a range of methodologies, including empirical studies, surveys, and experiments. Paper 3 uses a corpus of Reddit posts to compare the distribution of probabilities output by the OpenAI GPT-2 detection model. Paper 4 applies different scaling strategies and prompting techniques to evaluate the performance of Vision-Language Models (VLMs). Paper 5 conducts a conjoint survey experiment in seven countries to investigate public preferences for regulating AI. The use of empirical data and experimental designs allows researchers to test hypotheses and draw conclusions about the behavior and impact of AI systems.

### Research Gaps
Despite the advancements in AI research, several gaps remain. One gap is the lack of understanding about the long-term implications of AI on human society, including its potential impact on employment, education, and social relationships. Another gap is the need for more diverse and representative datasets to train AI models, particularly in areas such as natural language processing and computer vision. Additionally, there is a need for more research on the ethical and moral implications of AI, including the development of frameworks for responsible AI development and deployment.

### Future Directions
Future research in AI should focus on addressing the gaps and challenges identified in this review. One direction is to investigate the potential applications of AI in various domains, such as healthcare, education, and environmental sustainability. Another direction is to develop more transparent and explainable AI models, particularly in areas such as decision-making and prediction. Additionally, there is a need for more research on the governance and regulation of AI, including the development of frameworks for ensuring accountability and safety in AI systems.

### Conclusion
In conclusion, the current research in AI is diverse and rapidly evolving. The five papers reviewed in this study highlight the need for a multidisciplinary approach to understanding the impact and implications of AI. By addressing the research gaps and challenges identified in this review, future research can contribute to the development of more responsible and beneficial AI systems that align with human values and priorities. Ultimately, the goal of AI research should be to create systems that augment human capabilities, improve societal outcomes, and promote a more equitable and sustainable future.
